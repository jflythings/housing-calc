📍 How to Check Census Tract Eligibility for Any Address
1
Go to the FFIEC Geocoder
Visit ffiec.gov/geocode — this is the official federal tool NACA uses to determine census tract income levels.
2
Enter the property address
Type in the full street address of the home you're evaluating. Use the current year's HMDA file when prompted.
3
Find the tract income level (6th field)
Look for Tract Income Level in the results. It will say one of: Low, Moderate, Middle, or Upper. Low and Moderate are Priority Areas — Non-Priority members can buy here. Middle and Upper require Priority membership.
4
Cross-reference with your Priority status
If you are a Priority member (income below AMI) — you can buy anywhere, no tract check needed. If you are a Non-Priority member — you need the tract to show Low or Moderate. Middle or Upper tracts are not eligible for you.
5
Verify AMI threshold with NACA directly
The AMI number on the NACA website is updated periodically. Before making any decisions, confirm your current AMI threshold at naca.com by entering the target zip code. The number shown there is what NACA will use — not Census or HUD published figures.
