1
Right Now — Fix the Foundation
Credit, income, and NACA eligibility · Do these before anything else
✓
NowPull all three credit reports
Visit annualcreditreport.com — free, no card required. Pull Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion for both of you. Look for errors, collections, and high utilization. This is the starting point for everything else.
NowAdd him as authorized user on her best credit card
Her card with the longest history and lowest utilization. Call the issuer and request to add him as an authorized user — he does not need to use the card. Her payment history and credit age appear on his report within 30–60 days. Expected score improvement: 40–80 points in 3–6 months.
NowPay revolving balances below 10% utilization
Credit utilization is the fastest-moving score factor. Get every card below 30% immediately, below 10% before applying for a loan. Even a $500 paydown on a maxed $2,000 card moves the needle significantly.
NowGet his exact gross salary and W-2 Box 1 number
The difference between gross pay and W-2 Box 1 (after pre-tax deductions) determines NACA Priority status. If W-2 Box 1 is under $83,500 for Luzerne County — or his gross minus healthcare is under the AMI for whatever zip code they target — he qualifies as Priority, unlocking the 5.25% rate and removing census tract restrictions.
NowCheck CHIP eligibility for the baby
The baby likely qualifies for Pennsylvania CHIP (Children's Health Insurance Program) regardless of his income. This is free or near-free comprehensive pediatric coverage. If approved, remove the baby from his employer plan — this reduces his pre-tax deduction, potentially helping NACA priority math, and saves money.
NowDo NOT apply for any new credit
No new credit cards, car loans, store cards, or anything else until after the mortgage closes. Every hard inquiry drops the score 5–10 points. Rate shopping for a mortgage is fine — all mortgage inquiries within a 14-day window count as one inquiry.
SoonAttend the free NACA Homebuyer Workshop
Free, 4–5 hours, available online. Required first step before NACA counseling. Register at naca.com. No commitment. The workshop explains the full process, eligibility rules, and what documents you'll need. Do this before falling in love with any specific property.
SoonBecome NACA members ($36/year)
After the workshop, pay the $36 annual household membership. This starts the clock on your qualification timeline — NACA requires 3 months of documented savings and payment history from the date of membership. The sooner you join, the sooner the clock starts.
2
Months 1–3 — Location & Savings
Lock in the target market · Save aggressively · NACA qualification clock running
Month 1Decide: Columbus, Charlotte, or Wyoming PA
Run the calculator with real listings from each market. Columbus has the strongest case: top growth market, Ohio income tax only 2.75%, affordable entry prices, and likely NACA-eligible census tracts. Charlotte is compelling but $325k stretches the income at 31% DTI. Wyoming PA is the safe, low-risk option — best if staying near family is the real priority. Use the calculator to test the actual numbers, not gut feel.
Month 1Check NACA AMI for target zip codes
Go to naca.com and enter the zip codes of your top target areas. Write down the AMI number shown. Compare to his gross income (minus pre-tax healthcare). This determines Priority vs Non-Priority status and which properties are eligible. Different cities have different AMIs — Columbus and Charlotte may have very different thresholds than Luzerne County.
Month 1Find a NACA-experienced buyer's agent in the target city
NACA has a list of affiliated agents who understand the program timeline and requirements. A standard agent unfamiliar with NACA may actually hurt you — they'll push you toward conventional loans or mismanage the seller contribution conversation. Ask specifically: "Have you closed a NACA transaction in the past 12 months?"
Month 1–3Save every dollar — target $1,500–2,000/month
NACA requires 3 months of documented savings showing consistent monthly contributions. The amount matters less than the consistency — they want to see a pattern. Every dollar saved also goes toward the rate buydown at closing: 4 points per 1% rate reduction, max 5 points total. On a $250k loan, the max 5-point buydown costs $12,500 and saves roughly $150/month forever.
Month 1–3Schedule NACA counseling appointment
After the workshop and membership, book a counseling session. The counselor reviews income, debts, credit, and savings to determine qualification. They'll tell you exactly what needs to be fixed and how long it will take. NACA is known for being thorough — this process takes about 3 months total but the outcome is a genuinely below-market rate with no down payment.
Month 2–3Run target addresses through FFIEC geocoder
For every property you get seriously interested in, check the census tract income level at ffiec.cfpb.gov. If Non-Priority: need Low or Moderate tract. If Priority: no restriction. Do this before getting emotionally attached to a listing — it takes 30 seconds and can save weeks of wasted time.
3
Month 3–6 — Find the House
NACA qualified · Active search · Offer and negotiate
Month 3+Get NACA qualification letter
After counseling and 3 months of documented savings, NACA issues a qualification letter. This is equivalent to a pre-approval but carries more weight with sellers — NACA is a real lender with Bank of America behind it, not a broker. The letter states the maximum loan amount and the rate.
Month 3+Tour properties with the NACA-experienced agent
Focus on: natural gas or heat pump heat (avoid electric resistance or oil), no HOA or HOA with verified healthy financials, no basement moisture, out of flood zone, property tax bill verified from county auditor records (not Redfin), and ceiling price that keeps PITI under 31% of gross. Walk neighborhoods at different times of day.
OfferNegotiate seller contribution toward rate buydown
When making an offer, ask the seller to contribute toward the NACA rate buydown as part of negotiation. On a $250k home, each 1% of seller contribution (1 point) reduces your rate by 0.25%. Combined with your own savings, you can hit the 5-point maximum and get the rate as low as 3.75%–4.00%, saving $100–150/month for the life of the loan. Frame it as "in lieu of a price reduction" — it doesn't affect the appraised value.
OfferGet a specialist inspection for any basement moisture
Standard home inspectors underqualify basement water issues. For any property showing moisture, painted white foundation walls, or efflorescence — hire a waterproofing specialist (many offer free assessments) before finalizing the offer. Get the written report. Surface cosmetic moisture vs. active hydrostatic pressure intrusion is the difference between a $2,000 fix and a $20,000 problem. Walk away from the latter.
OfferAsk seller about property tax exemptions in writing
Homestead exemptions, senior discounts, and veteran exemptions don't transfer to a new buyer. The tax bill you see on Redfin may reflect the seller's exemptions — your bill after closing could be higher. Ask the agent to request in writing what exemptions the current owner carries, then verify the actual millage rate with the county auditor to calculate your real bill.
ClosingNACA closes within 28 days of contract
NACA's timeline from accepted offer to closing is typically 28 days. Have all documents ready: 2 years tax returns, 2 months pay stubs, 2 months bank statements, and the qualification letter. No surprises — NACA pre-approves thoroughly, so if you're qualified, the closing is smooth.
4
Years 1–5 — Build Toward Sacramento
In the house · She returns to work · Sacramento comes into view
OngoingShe returns to work — add income to savings, not lifestyle
When she returns to work, the household income jumps significantly. Bank her entire paycheck. At $90k combined they can save $2,000–3,000/month. At $130–140k combined Sacramento becomes actively viable via NACA — run the NACA Analyzer with the new income number to see when Sacramento unlocks.
OngoingKeep him as sole NACA borrower until refinance or Sacramento
NACA restricts: no household member 21+ can own other property at Sacramento closing. The interim house must be sold before or at Sacramento closing. Plan the sale timeline 3–6 months in advance of the Sacramento purchase to avoid bridge loan complications.
Year 3–4Monitor Sacramento NACA AMI threshold
Sacramento's HUD AMI is significantly higher than Luzerne County — likely $95,000–110,000+ for a household of 3. At $130–140k combined income they'll be Non-Priority, but Sacramento has ample low/moderate income tracts at affordable price points. Run the NACA Analyzer with Sacramento zip codes and their projected combined income to find the window.
Year 4–5Sell interim home → Sacramento
List 3–6 months before the target Sacramento move date. In a Columbus or Charlotte growth market, net proceeds after 5 years at 4–5% appreciation should be $50,000–$80,000+. Combined with 5 years of savings, this is the Sacramento down payment stack. Do not sell without having the Sacramento NACA qualification letter already in hand — you don't want to be sitting on proceeds in a HYSA longer than necessary.
Year 5Open enrollment — fix healthcare before Sacramento
California has Covered California with strong ACA subsidies. Once they have a Sacramento move date, review healthcare options well in advance. A move to California is a qualifying life event that triggers a Special Enrollment Period — they don't have to wait for open enrollment. At $140k household income, subsidies on Covered California will be modest but available.
Remember: The NACA $25,000 soft-second lien on the interim property requires owner-occupancy for the life of the NACA loan. This lien must be resolved (typically released upon sale) before Sacramento closing. Build this into the timeline — confirm the payoff process with NACA at least 6 months before the planned sale.
